Why Summer School

The idea for Children’s Creativity and Innovation Workshop emanated from the six years’ experience of our national IGNITE programme. We wanted to do more than just recognising the awardees. We wanted to imbibe in them certain values. Hence the thought of conducting this workshop came into being. So, every year we bring together Ignite awardees and other children from rural and urban areas, mostly from schools for under privileged children, some from private schools and a few from slum areas who are generally un-schooled. The basic idea of the children workshop is to solve the problems of children by the children and sensitise them about the socio-economic challenges prevailing in the society they live in. In the process, we get to dive into a pool of innovative yet commonsensical solutions for complex problems, which don’t come into the learned minds of their matured counter parts. It’s a humbling experience which reaffirms and demonstrates the fact that, “Small minds can solve Big Problems”.

What at Summer School

The objective of Children Workshop is to develop an operational framework for empowering children to not only articulate their problems but also to find solutions both individually and collectively. This programme helps us to understand how young children can be a significant part of the national innovation value chain.The focus of the Children Workshop is to support children in developing innovative ideas to solve basic problems faced by underprivileged people. The IGNITE children from different parts of the country, who already have developed patentable ideas from their own experiences, are asked to apply their skills to finding solutions for slum dwellers, alongside children whose lives are close to the experiences of slum dwellers.

Process

Children have an innate quality of questioning everything around them. They sometimes come up with very simple yet unique ideas. Inspired by this quality of young minds every year Children Workshop is organised. It’s a two day workshop where children from every socio-economic strata come together and participate. On the first day of the workshop an orientation session is organised where the children are made aware about how there prospective ideas matter and how ideas from previous workshop have created an impact and brought about a change. This helps in creating confidence among them that their ideas matter and are valued. After their pristine and curious minds are warmed up, they are formed into random groups and taken out for field visits to the different parts of the city having underprivileged communities. On the second day, they work in groups, brainstorm, sketch their problems and respective solutions, after which they present their ideas to everyone present.

Children Workshop 2015 at Rashtrapati Bhawan

In March 2015, the Children’s Creativity Workshop was held at Rashtrapati Bhawan alongside the Festival of Innovation event. It sought solutions to societal problems in slums around Delhi. Children Workshop 2014 at IIM A

In November 2014, a two and half day Children’s Creativity Workshop was organised at Indian Institute of Management (IIM), Ahmedabad campus. It brought together the 28 Ignite awardees and about 40 other children from rural and urban areas, both from privileged and underprivileged background.
